WHO ARE WE?

We at Farmer’s Daughter Crop Connections, are a group of young agro-entrepreneurs (farmers, producers, suppliers, educators, etc.) who each are a part of and huld a stake in the well-being of Food and Nutrition. As stakehulders possessing individual skills and talents, we cullaborate with each other to maintain a food secure local market and environment.

OUR MISSION

To achieve product(s), good(s) and/or service(s) that seek to promote or adhere to Food Security and Local Market production.

  • Services/Products rendered must be:
  • Available- must be accessible and affordable.
  • Having Agency- must be reliable and reputable as a product/service in its applied area.
  • Safe/Secure- must be uncompromising to human health (clean and inclusive of food preparation procedure) and culture (religion and lifestyle practices).

2025: Plan & Design Product Concept

To meet P/D Product Conceptualization:

  • Pitch potential ideas – must align with goals.
  • Perform research – to flesh out what is known and what is needed for proof of concept.
  • Test ideas – assess whether the idea is feasible through physical sampling and review of known alternatives/attempts.
  • Proof of Concept – a physical product that is by some means achievable (efficiency is unimportant at this stage).

2026

Prototype Development & Consumer Testing

  • Refine prototype – improve product design and usability based on concept results.
  • Conduct consumer testing – gather feedback on taste, texture, appearance, and market appeal.
  • Evaluate competitive products – compare the prototype to market leaders and niche alternatives.
  • Revise formula or presentation – adjust the prototype based on consumer insight and technical assessment.

2027: 1st Batch Release

To meet Batch Release:

  • Meet shelf/market requirements – product must meet in-house environmental requirements.
  • Nutrition analysis of product – assessment of the chemical properties within product.
  • Safety regulations – reinventing formula to best meet food safety standards of the consumer base.
  • Mass Production – process outline and utilization of outline to efficiently process material into product for shelf use.

2028: Line Development

To meet Line Development:

  • Product Alteration – produce variety based on formula editing to meet a wider market.
  • Research and Development – create several revised concepts with Batch Release standards.
  • Revised Production and Concept – reassess input and output standards to efficiently produce a variety of similar goods (variety may be in: flavour – having different bases, time – having varieties available for different cultures, etc.)
